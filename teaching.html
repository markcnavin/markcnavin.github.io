Teaching

I teach philosophy, bioethics, and medical humanities at both the undergraduate and medical school levels.

At Oakland University, I have taught Introduction to Philosophy, Introduction to Ethics, Political Philosophy, Philosophy of Law, and Global Justice, among other courses. I have also designed and taught Honors College seminars on topics including science denialism and democracy, community and moral progress, and the ethics and practice of science. At the Oakland University William Beaumont School of Medicine, I am a course instructor for Medical Humanities and Clinical Bioethics 3, where I lead seven-week seminars in pediatric ethics, public health ethics, and vaccine ethics. I also teach sessions in the first- and second-year medical humanities and clinical bioethics courses.

I supervise undergraduate honors theses and serve as a research mentor for medical students in the OUWB Embark/Capstone program. Several of these collaborations have led to coauthored publications.
