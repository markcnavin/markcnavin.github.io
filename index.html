Mark C. Navin

PhD, BSN, RN, HEC-C

I am a philosopher, bioethicist, healthcare ethics consultant, and nurse.

I am Professor of Philosophy and Department Chair at Oakland University, Lecturer in Foundational Medical Studies at the Oakland University William Beaumont School of Medicine, and both a Healthcare Ethics Consultant and Clinical Nurse at Corewell Health William Beaumont University Hospital. These roles inform each other and shape everything I do: my scholarship, my teaching, my clinical practice, and my service to institutions and communities.

I have a PhD in philosophy from the University of Pennsylvania and a BSN from Oakland University. I am a certified Healthcare Ethics Consultant (HEC-C) and a licensed registered nurse in the state of Michigan.

My work connects philosophical inquiry to the practical and embodied realities of medicine, nursing, and public health. After years of writing about bioethics, I wanted to test my ideas against the complexities that clinicians face every day. I became a practicing healthcare ethics consultant, doing case consultation, ethics education, policy development, and committee leadership at a large health system. More recently, I completed a nursing degree, passed the NCLEX, and began working as a bedside nurse. I wanted to understand healthcare ethics not just as a consultant who arrives when called, but as a clinician who works inside the system.

My research focuses on vaccine ethics and policy, clinical ethics, and public health ethics. I have published two monographs, Values and Vaccine Refusal (Routledge, 2016) and America's New Vaccine Wars (Oxford University Press, 2023, with Katie Attwell), along with three edited volumes and nearly a hundred articles and book chapters. My work appears in journals including JAMA, JAMA Pediatrics, Pediatrics, Milbank Quarterly, Hastings Center Report, and the American Journal of Bioethics.
